Portfolio is adding to Alibaba (BABA) between $152 and $156 as this week should bring upward estimate revisions after the annual Investor Day event which concludes today.
 
I've been following the reports out of Hangzhou, China during Alibaba's two-day Investor Event and I like what I'm hearing. While the company gave no update on guidance from their last projection -- 60% revenue growth -- many key initiatives are unfolding better than expected including...
 
1) increased monetization of online assets like Taobao
2) crossplatform synergy in traffic, promotion & data across core commerce and local lifestyle service to expand the 600 million base of customers
3) Hema & Ele.me grocery expansion where their logistics mastery drives cost efficiency
4) New Retail capitalizing on platform with 70% of net additions originating from lower tier cities
5) recently launched 88VIP membership program ties together customer rewards and benefits across breadth of services under the Alibaba umbrella
 
Additionally, Jack Ma may help put in a bottom soon with this extreme realism about the current trade battles. This morning at the event, Ma told investors to hunker down for a long, drawn out trade war between the United States and China...
 
"It's going to last long, it's going to be a mess." 
 
20 years long, to be exact.
 
Now that sounds like an epic war.
 
While the tech billionaire dismissed speculation that geopolitical headwinds played a part in the surprise announcement of his retirement last week, he acknowledged that the ongoing spat with China's largest trade partner is hurting Alibaba's business.
 
Ma said a lot of businesses in China and the United States will be in trouble in the short term, and eventually, Chinese firms will be forced to turn to other countries.
 
Ma said the trade war is "against China," but also criticized his country's policies, urging leaders to view the pain inflicted by Trump's tariffs as an opportunity to "upgrade."
 
"China must open the market," he added.
 
This honesty is welcome from an entrepreneur who owes much of his massive success to support from the Chinese government.
 
Ma also took the opportunity to reassure investors that the $410 billion company he helped build is in safe hands. He announced last week that he will step down from Alibaba in a year's time, handing over the reins to CEO Daniel Zhang.
 
"I'm 100% sure Daniel will do a better job than I do," Ma said.
